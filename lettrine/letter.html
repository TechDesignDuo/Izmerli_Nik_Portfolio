Architecture and Typography Combined


This lettrine was created to represent a Renaissance Exhibition at the Montreal Museum of Fine Arts. My aim was to create an eye-catching letter, that was inspired by classical Italian architecture and Renaissance art.

Rome Wasn't Built in a Day



Blueprint


The base of this piece consists of lines, shapes and pen-drawn objects. This skeleton was drawn in Adobe Illustrator. With the foundation set up, this illustration was ready to receive depth.

Color and Texture


I achieved the 3D look through the use of opposing gradients between the column spaces and gaps. I added a grainy texture to give the whole pillar a more realistic look. The ellipse and curve were joined together before being filled with a shining gradient consisting of yellow and brown palettes.

Completing the Look


In order to finalize this piece, I masked the pillar to allow the golden arches to pass through, followed by the addition of a hand-drawn shadow. The result is a stunning lettrine that has depth and character (pun intended).
